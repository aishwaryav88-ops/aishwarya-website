About

I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Illinois Chicago. My work combines computational modeling, network science, optimization, and data-driven analysis to understand information flow in large-scale networks, especially structural and functional brain connectivity data. I also work on prompt engineering and LLM-based approaches for extracting structured information from unstructured text.

Research

Research Interests

Brain Connectivity & Consciousness

Modeling structural and functional connectomes to study coordination, congestion, and communication patterns across brain states.

Network Optimization Models

Applying traffic-assignment ideas such as System Optimum and User Equilibrium to understand global versus local optimization in biological networks.

LLM Information Extraction

Designing prompt engineering strategies for converting natural-language text into reliable structured data for downstream analytical workflows.
